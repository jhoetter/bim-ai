The target is a minimalist two-level house with a 14 m by 10 m plan logic. The front is the long south edge. The ground floor is a vertically clad rectangular base, but its right bay is recessed as a carport/covered void. The upper level is visually dominant: a smooth white folded wrapper spans the full width, projects past the cladded base, and forms a thick frame around the front upper loggia.

The upper front facade is set back about 1.9 m from the front edge. It reads as a deep loggia rather than an applied balcony. The loggia has a three-bay composition: left glass, a central vertical cladding pier, and right glass. A thin dark guard rail crosses the opening near the front edge.

The roof is a white asymmetric gable/folded shell, not a dark roof placed on a box. The shell has visible thickness at the front and side edges. A large open-to-sky roof court/terrace void sits in the right/back portion of the upper plan and must be visible in high/front-right views. The roof void needs a floor, white return faces, glazing, and guard/edge treatment so it reads as an occupied courtyard/terrace.

The floorplan is authoritative for scale and room topology. Ground floor: kitchen/dining and living occupy the left/central open plan; entry and straight stair sit near the south middle-right; bath/laundry and utility are north-east; the carport/garage void occupies the south-east/right bay. Upper floor: primary suite is west/north-west, ensuite/closet west/south-west, landing and stair center, bedroom 2 east/south-east, roof court east/north-east, and a continuous deep south loggia spans most of the upper front.

Non-negotiable visible features:

- 14 m wide, 10 m deep modern house proportions.
- Smaller cladded ground support with a recessed right-front carport/covered void.
- Larger white upper wrapper with front/right cantilever and thick shell edges.
- Deep recessed upper front loggia with black rail and three-bay facade.
- Large right/back roof-court void cut through the white roof shell.
- Large ground and upper glazing with visible stair behind the front glass.
- Vertical cladding zones on ground base and central upper pier.
